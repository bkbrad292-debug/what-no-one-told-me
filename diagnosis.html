The Moment I Found Out I Had Prostate Cancer

The moment of truth didn’t come in a doctor’s office. It came from an email.

The Email

Like a lot of things these days, the news didn’t come from a phone call.

I got an email saying my biopsy results were available.

The moment of truth was sitting right in front of me.

What I Expected

I logged into my Virginia Urology account expecting good news.

After all, I thought I was doing everything right. I stayed active. I paid attention to my health. I took the supplements that were supposed to support my prostate.

I wasn’t supposed to be a statistic.

The Shock

Then I started reading.

Cancer.

Gleason 3+4=7.

And then another detail that made it even more serious:

cribriform carcinoma.

That was the shock of my life.

Telling My Wife

I was off work that day, so I had to walk upstairs and tell my wife.

She was in shock too—for the same reasons I was.

We weren’t expecting this.

Telling Everyone Else

To save time, I sent a group text to my family because I knew the calls would start coming.

And they did.

My mom. My dad, who was also dealing with cancer himself. My brother. My sons.

Then came fraternity brothers, friends, and coworkers.

Once you say it out loud enough times, it becomes real in a whole different way.

What Happened Next

After that came the appointments, the research, and the decisions.

Surgery or radiation. Risks. Side effects. Outcomes.

But that first moment—reading those results on a screen—is the one I’ll never forget.

What I Wish I Knew

Nobody really prepares you for how surreal it feels to read news like that by yourself.

There’s no soft landing.

One minute you’re hoping everything is fine. The next minute, life has changed.

Final Thought

If you’ve just gotten that kind of news, take a breath.

The shock is real. The fear is real. The questions are real.

But you do not have to figure it all out in one moment.

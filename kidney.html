Unexpected turn

When I Thought It Was Over… Another Diagnosis

I thought I was moving forward. Then one scan changed everything again.

The Scan That Changed Everything

On March 11, 2026, I went in for another scan to check my urinary system.

It was supposed to be routine—just making sure everything looked good after surgery.

Like most scans, it was quick and uneventful.

By the time I got home and ate something, I already had an email saying my results were ready.

Some Relief First

I opened the results and started reading.

My bladder looked okay. My urethra looked normal. No signs of infection.

There was something noted in the prostate bed, but it appeared to be normal post-surgical changes.

That was a relief.

Then… Another Shock

Then I kept reading.

A Bosniak 4 renal cyst on my right kidney.

These types of cysts are cancerous the majority of the time—around 85% or more.

And it didn’t appear to be related to my prostate cancer.

I just sat there and said, “WTF?”

Starting Over… Again

Just like that, I was right back where I had been before.

I had to tell my family, my friends, my fraternity brothers—all over again—that I most likely had another cancer.

It felt like starting over.

Timing That Made It Harder

Two days later was my birthday.

March 13, 2026—I turned 57.

We had family and a couple of close friends over for dinner, but in the back of my mind, I knew what I was facing.

The Plan Moving Forward

At my next appointment, my surgeon was straightforward as always.

These cysts aren’t typically biopsied at this stage because of how often they are cancerous.

The plan was surgery—a partial nephrectomy to remove the cyst.

The expectation was that I would still retain about 80–90% kidney function.

I would return to work for a short time… then go right back out again for another surgery.

The Mental Side

Mentally, this was tough.

I had just gone through prostate cancer surgery.

I thought I was moving forward.

And now I was facing another diagnosis and another surgery.

Final Thought

One of the hardest parts of this journey is thinking you’ve reached the end—only to realize you haven’t.

But just like the first time, the only option is to move forward.

One step at a time.
